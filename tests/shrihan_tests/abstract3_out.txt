We present updated observational constraints on the spatially flat \phiCDM cosmological model, in which dark energy is described by a minimally coupled scalar field phi with an inverse power-law potential energy density V equals V sub 0 phi to the power of -alpha. Using a combination of Planck 2018 cosmic microwave background (CMB) temperature, polarization (P18), and lensing power spectra (lensing), along with a comprehensive compilation of non-CMB data including baryon acoustic oscillation, type Ia supernova, Hubble parameter, and growth rate measurements, we analyze parameter constraints of the \phiCDM and \phiCDM+A sub L models where A sub L is the CMB lensing consistency parameter. We find that the scalar field parameter alpha, which governs dark energy dynamics, is more tightly constrained by non-CMB data than by CMB data alone. From P18+lensing+non-CMB data, we obtain alpha equals 0.055 plus or minus 0.041 in the \phiCDM model and alpha equals 0.095 plus or minus 0.056 in the \phiCDM+A sub L model, mildly favoring evolving dark energy over a cosmological constant by 1.3sigma and 1.7sigma. The estimated Hubble constant is H naught equals 67.55sub -0.46 to the power of +0.53 km s to the -1 megaparsecs to the -1 for P18+lensing+non-CMB data in the \phiCDM model, consistent with median statistics and some local determinations, but in tension with other local determinations. The constraints for matter density and clustering amplitude (omega sub m equals 0.3096 plus or minus 0.0055, sigma eight equals 0.8013sub -0.0067 to the power of +0.0077) of the flat \phiCDM model statistically agree with Lambda C D M model values. Allowing the CMB lensing consistency parameter A sub L to vary reduces tensions between CMB and non-CMB data, although we find A sub L equals 1.105 plus or minus 0.037, 2.8sigma higher than unity, consistent with the excess smoothing seen in Planck data. Model comparison using AIC and DIC shows that the \phiCDM model provides a fit comparable to Lambda C D M, with the \phiCDM+A sub L extension slightly preferred in some cases. Overall, our results indicate that while the Lambda C D M model remains an excellent fit current data leave open the possibility of mildly evolving quintessence-like dynamical dark energy, without a phantom-divide crossing.
